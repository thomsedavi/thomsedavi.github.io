Project: Lego head finder

Have you ever wanted to source a specific Lego head, for example one with an eyepatch and stubble, but didn't want to search through the 1,000+ Lego heads in the database? This tool should make it easier for you.

Difficulty: Easy? University project level difficulty.

Time required: Once the project had been set up, it would require a lot of time to catalogue every single lego head ever, and then maintain that database with new heads :(

Motivation: The Lego community seems to get on okay without this particular tool? But as a Lego fan I have often thought how useful a tool like this might be.

Project: Learn a language by teaching it to a robot.

Rather more ambitious! A kind of reverse language learning idea, where you learn by teaching. A robot will ask, 'what's the French word for Car', for example, and you do the research yourself. Later on you quiz the robot and correct it when it suggests that Papillon means Car. Like I said, ambitious, especially if you tried teaching it grammar as well...

Difficulty: Potentially super difficult.

Time required: Could be very quick to begin with, but slowly descend into a spiral of madness.

Motivation: I think this would be a great tool for me, but would it have any value to other people?

Project: Driving game of crazy architecture.

After being inspired by a series of photographs of peculiar and interesting architecture that could never exist in the real world, I realised that what I want to do is drive around in them. There wouldn't be much else to the game. There are existing driving game engines I could use, but I'd have to teach myself to design in 3D, which could be fun.

Difficulty: Probably quite difficult.

Time required: Designing entire landscapes could be quite time intensive, but people do it in minecraft all the time, so who knows?

Motivation: I actually really want to do this, as a kind of art installation in a game.
